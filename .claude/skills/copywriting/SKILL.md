# Copywriting

Write marketing copy that is clear, compelling, and drives action.

---

## Step 1: Gather Context

**Check for product marketing context first.** If `.claude/product-marketing-context.md` exists, read it before asking questions.

Gather what's missing across four areas:

1. **Page purpose:** What type of page? (homepage, landing page, pricing, feature, about) What is the ONE primary action visitors should take?
2. **Audience:** Who is the ideal customer? What problem are they solving? What objections or hesitations do they have? What language do they use to describe their problem?
3. **Product/offer:** What are you selling? What makes it different? What's the key transformation or outcome? Any proof points — numbers, testimonials, case studies?
4. **Context:** Where is traffic coming from (ads, organic, email)? What do visitors already know before arriving?

---

## Step 2: Choose the Right Approach by Page Type

**Decision: What type of page is this?**

### Homepage
Serve multiple audiences without being generic. Lead with the broadest value proposition. Provide clear paths for different visitor intents (prospect, customer, partner, job seeker). Headline should work for cold traffic that knows nothing about you.

**Structure:**
1. Above-fold: value prop + primary CTA
2. Social proof (logos or stats — fast credibility)
3. Problem/pain (show you understand their situation)
4. Solution/benefits (3-5 key outcomes, not features)
5. How it works (3-4 steps — reduce perceived complexity)
6. Objection handling (FAQ, comparisons, or guarantees)
7. Final CTA (recap value, repeat CTA, risk reversal)

### Landing Page
Single message, single CTA. Match headline exactly to the ad or email that sent traffic here. Complete the argument on one page — no navigation, no distractions.

**Structure:**
1. Headline matching traffic source message
2. Subheadline expanding the promise
3. Primary CTA above the fold
4. Brief proof (testimonial or stat)
5. Benefits (not features)
6. Objection handling
7. Final CTA

### Pricing Page
Help visitors choose the right plan. Address "which is right for me?" anxiety directly. Make the recommended plan visually obvious. Include an FAQ section handling price objections.

**Structure:**
1. Headline framing value, not cost
2. Plan comparison (3 tiers max, middle highlighted)
3. Feature matrix
4. FAQ (common price objections)
5. Social proof from paying customers
6. CTA per plan

### Feature Page
Connect feature → benefit → outcome. Show use cases and concrete examples. Include a clear path to try or buy.

**Structure:**
1. Headline: outcome the feature enables (not the feature name)
2. Subheadline: who this is for
3. How it works (show, don't just tell)
4. Use cases (2-3 specific scenarios)
5. Social proof specific to this feature
6. CTA

### About Page
Tell the story of why you exist. Connect the mission to customer benefit. Still include a CTA — this page gets high-intent traffic.

## Step 3: Write the Copy

### Headline Formulas (Use One)

- `{Achieve outcome} without {pain point}`
- `The {category} for {audience}`
- `Never {unpleasant event} again`
- `{Question highlighting main pain point}`
- `{Specific outcome} in {timeframe}`

For comprehensive headline formulas: See [references/copy-frameworks.md](references/copy-frameworks.md)

### Subheadline
Expand on the headline. Add specificity. 1-2 sentences max.

### CTA Copy
**Avoid:** Submit, Sign Up, Learn More, Click Here, Get Started

**Use:** Action verb + what they get + qualifier

- "Start My Free Trial"
- "Get the Complete Checklist"
- "See Pricing for My Team"
- "Create Your First [Thing]"

For natural transition phrases between sections: See [references/natural-transitions.md](references/natural-transitions.md)

## Writing Rules — Apply to Every Section

**Clarity over cleverness.** If you must choose, choose clear.

**Benefits over features.** Features = what it does. Benefits = what that means for the customer.

**Specificity over vagueness.**
- Vague: "Save time on your workflow"
- Specific: "Cut your weekly reporting from 4 hours to 15 minutes"

**Customer language over company language.** Use words customers use. Mirror voice-of-customer from reviews, interviews, and support tickets.

**One idea per section.** Each section advances one argument. Build a logical flow down the page.

**Word-level rules:**
- Simple over complex: "use" not "utilize," "help" not "facilitate"
- Active over passive: "We generate reports" not "Reports are generated"
- Confident over qualified: remove "almost," "very," "really"
- Show over tell: describe the outcome instead of using adverbs
- No fabricated statistics or testimonials

**Quick quality check — scan for these before delivering:**
- Jargon that could confuse outsiders?
- Sentences doing too much?
- Passive voice constructions?
- Exclamation points? (remove them)
- Marketing buzzwords without substance? ("streamline," "optimize," "innovative," "leverage")

For thorough line-by-line review, use the **copy-editing** skill after drafting.

## Voice and Tone

Before writing, establish:

**Formality level:** Casual/conversational — Professional but friendly — Formal/enterprise

**Brand personality:** Playful or serious? Bold or understated? Technical or accessible?

Maintain consistency, but vary intensity: headlines can be bolder, body copy should be clearer, CTAs should be action-oriented.

---

## Rhetorical Techniques (Use When Appropriate)

**Rhetorical questions** — Engage readers by making them think about their own situation.
- "Hate returning stuff to Amazon?"
- "Tired of chasing approvals?"

**Analogies** — Make abstract concepts concrete and memorable.

**Direct address** — "you" beats passive constructions everywhere.

**Humor** — Only if it fits the brand and doesn't undermine clarity.

---

## Output Format

Deliver copy in three parts:

**1. Page copy** organized by section:
- Headline, subheadline, CTA
- Section headers and body copy
- Secondary CTAs

**2. Annotations** on key choices:
- Why you made this decision
- What principle it applies

**3. Alternatives** for headlines and CTAs:
- Option A: [copy] — [rationale]
- Option B: [copy] — [rationale]
- Option C: [copy] — [rationale]

**4. Meta content** (if relevant):
- Page title (for SEO)
- Meta description
