## Platform Decision Rules

Apply these rules immediately when a platform is specified. Do not defer to reference files.

### LinkedIn
- **Audience:** B2B professionals, decision-makers, founders
- **Post length:** 150–300 words for standard posts; carousels 5–10 slides
- **Tone:** Professional but human — insight-driven, not corporate
- **Format rules:**
  - Open with a single bold hook line (no preamble)
  - Use short paragraphs (1–3 lines), never long blocks
  - Put the CTA or punchline in the last 2 lines
  - Avoid external links in the post body — put links in comments
  - Carousels outperform single images; use for frameworks and lists
- **Post types that work:** Story posts, contrarian takes, frameworks, lessons learned, data-backed insights
- **Frequency:** 3–5x per week
- **Avoid:** Excessive hashtags (2–3 max), engagement bait questions with no substance

### Twitter/X
- **Audience:** Tech, founders, operators, real-time conversation
- **Post length:** Tweets under 280 characters; threads 5–15 tweets
- **Tone:** Sharp, direct, casual — low friction to read
- **Format rules:**
  - First tweet of a thread must stand alone as a hook
  - End threads with a clear summary or CTA tweet
  - Hot takes and contrarian views get outsized reach
  - Real-time commentary on news/trends performs well
- **Post types that work:** Threads of lessons, hot takes, number lists, live reactions
- **Frequency:** 3–10x per day (mix of original posts and replies)
- **Avoid:** Threads that bury the point, posting links without commentary

### Instagram
- **Audience:** Visual-first, lifestyle, consumer and B2C brands, younger professionals
- **Post length:** Captions 100–150 words for feed; Stories are visual-first with minimal text
- **Tone:** Warm, aspirational, visually descriptive
- **Format rules:**
  - Lead with a strong visual — copy supports it, doesn't replace it
  - Carousels: hook with slide 1, deliver value across slides 2–8, CTA on last slide
  - Reels: hook in first 2 seconds, value in 30–60 seconds
  - Hashtags: 5–15, mix of niche and broad
- **Post types that work:** Carousels with step-by-step content, Reels with quick tips, Stories for behind-the-scenes
- **Frequency:** 1–2 feed posts/day + Stories daily
- **Avoid:** Text-heavy images, posting without a clear visual concept

### TikTok
- **Audience:** Broad, skews younger, high discovery potential
- **Post length:** 30–90 seconds optimal; hook in first 3 seconds
- **Tone:** Authentic, unpolished, energetic — perfection is a liability
- **Format rules:**
  - Hook must appear in first 2–3 seconds (spoken or on-screen text)
  - Use trending audio when relevant
  - Subtitles/captions always — most watch without sound
  - Pattern interrupts every 7–10 seconds to retain attention
- **Post types that work:** Tutorials, reactions, storytelling, POV formats, "day in the life"
- **Frequency:** 1–4x per day
- **Avoid:** Repurposing LinkedIn/Twitter content directly without reformatting for vertical video

### Facebook
- **Audience:** Communities, local businesses, 35+ demographic
- **Post length:** 40–80 words for feed; longer for Groups
- **Tone:** Conversational, community-oriented
- **Format rules:**
  - Native video significantly outperforms shared links
  - Groups drive more engagement than Pages
  - Ask questions to drive comments
- **Post types that work:** Group discussions, native video, community polls
- **Frequency:** 1–2x per day

## Content Pillars Framework

Build your content around 3–5 pillars that align with expertise and audience interests.

### Example for a SaaS Founder

| Pillar | % of Content | Topics |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Industry insights | 30% | Trends, data, predictions |
| Behind-the-scenes | 25% | Building the company, lessons learned |
| Educational | 25% | How-tos, frameworks, tips |
| Personal | 15% | Stories, values, hot takes |
| Promotional | 5% | Product updates, offers |

For each pillar, define: what unique perspective you have, what questions your audience asks, and what you can create consistently.

## Hook Formulas

The first line determines whether anyone reads the rest.

### Curiosity Hooks
- "I was wrong about [common belief]."
- "The real reason [outcome] happens isn't what you think."
- "[Impressive result] — and it only took [surprisingly short time]."

### Story Hooks
- "Last week, [unexpected thing] happened."
- "I almost [big mistake/failure]."
- "3 years ago, I [past state]. Today, [current state]."

### Value Hooks
- "How to [desirable outcome] (without [common pain]):"
- "[Number] [things] that [outcome]:"
- "Stop [common mistake]. Do this instead:"

### Contrarian Hooks
- "Unpopular opinion: [bold statement]"
- "[Common advice] is wrong. Here's why:"
- "I stopped [common practice] and [positive result]."

## Content Repurposing System

Turn one piece of content into many:

### Blog Post → Social Content

| Platform | Format |
|----------|--------|
| LinkedIn | Key insight + link in comments |
| LinkedIn | Carousel of main points |
| Twitter/X | Thread of key takeaways |
| Instagram | Carousel with visuals |
| Instagram | Reel summarizing the post |

### Repurposing Workflow

1. Create pillar content (blog, video, podcast)
2. Extract 3–5 key insights
3. Adapt to each platform's format and tone rules above
4. Schedule across the week to spread distribution
5. Reshare evergreen content after 60–90 days

1. Review content pillar topics
2. Write 5 LinkedIn posts
3. Write 3 Twitter threads + daily tweets
4. Create Instagram carousel + Reel ideas
5. Schedule everything
6. Leave room for real-time engagement

## Engagement Strategy

### Daily Engagement Routine (30 min)

1. Respond to all comments on your posts (5 min)
2. Comment on 5–10 posts from target accounts (15 min)
3. Share/repost with added insight (5 min)
4. Send 2–3 DMs to new connections (5 min)

### Quality Comments
- Add new insight, not just "Great post!"
- Share a related experience
- Ask a thoughtful follow-up question

## Analytics & Optimization

### Metrics That Matter

**Awareness:** Impressions, reach, follower growth rate

**Engagement:** Engagement rate, comments (higher value than likes), shares/reposts, saves

**Conversion:** Link clicks, profile visits, DMs received, leads attributed

### If Engagement Is Low
- Test new hooks
- Post at different times
- Try different formats
- Increase engagement with others first

### If Reach Is Declining
- Avoid external links in post body
- Increase posting frequency
- Engage more in comments
- Test video/visual content

---

## Scheduling: Schedule vs. Post Live

**Schedule:** Core content posts, threads, carousels, evergreen content

**Post live:** Real-time commentary, responses to news/trends, engagement with others

Maintain 1–2 weeks of scheduled content. Review queue weekly for relevance.

## Reverse Engineering Viral Content

1. Find 10–20 accounts with high engagement in your niche
2. Collect 500+ posts for analysis
3. Analyze patterns — hooks, formats, CTAs that work
4. Codify repeatable patterns
5. Apply patterns with your own voice
6. Bridge attention to business results
